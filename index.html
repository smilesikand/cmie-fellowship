FELLOWSHIP STREAMS

  • Principal Leadership Development Programme


    The PDL programme focuses on working with Heads of School in developing leadership and collaboration, instilling personal responsibility and a shared vision for school transformation through peer group learning. Fellows will support the execution of the programme as well as Principals as they bring back practice within their own schools working with teachers and School Management Committees.

  • Mentor Teacher Programme


    The Mentor Teacher programme has been designed to leverage the creative expertise of 200 teachers in further strengthening the academic and pedagogic capacities of other 45,000+ teachers of DoE schools. This will be achieved through cluster based learning, on strengthening their foundational skills and building perspective with regards to the learning capabilities of students as stated in the overall goal for the year.

  • SCERT Online Platform for Learning 


    The Government of Delhi is in process of creating an online platform for teachers to support them in strengthening several concepts and classroom management practices in the classrooms. This platform will engage teachers through extensively used tools for individualised training and learning. The SCERT Fellows will be working on developing content as well as the platform.

  • School Management Committee (SMC)

    SMC Fellows will strengthen the relationship between schools and parents in communities, create broader awareness on children’s needs, support teachers and assist Principals with the implementation of the School Learning Plan. Fellows will also work in developing material for the training and capacity building of SMCs and do field visits to document support ongoing work by SMCs.

  • Learning Assessment 


    Fellows engaged in learning assessment project would be responsible for creation and pilot runs of tools of learning assessment for Foundational Learning Skills programme. In addition, they’d be involved in gathering and analysis of the relevant data.

  • Monthly Information Systems


    MIS Fellows would be involved in projects to increase efficiency and help in better functioning of the processes within SCERT. This might include automating and organising frequent activities/processes, and formulating necessary documentation related to the projects run by the SCERT.

Desired Criteria
